Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 9, 2026

Aptron ("we", "the app") is a personal productivity dashboard. This policy explains what data Aptron handles, why, and the choices you have. Aptron is operated as a single-owner application; if you have questions, contact admin@aptron.com.

Information we collect

  • Account information. The email address and password you use to sign in, managed through our authentication provider (Supabase).
  • Content you create. Calendar time-blocks, goals, and the notes you enter into the dashboard's trackers.
  • Google Calendar data (only if you connect it). When you explicitly link your Google account, Aptron accesses your Google Calendar events and stores a Google authorization (refresh) token so it can keep syncing.

How we use your information

  • To display your calendar and dashboard, and to authenticate you.
  • To synchronize events between Aptron and your Google Calendar — reading your events to show them, and writing your Aptron blocks back to your calendar.

Aptron does not use your Google Calendar data for advertising, does not sell it, and does not share it with third parties except the infrastructure providers named below that are required to run the service.

Google API Services — Limited Use

Aptron's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Google Calendar data is used solely to provide and improve the calendar-sync feature you enabled, is not transferred to others except as necessary to provide that feature, is not used for advertising, and is not read by humans except with your consent or for security/legal reasons.

Storage and security

  • Your dashboard content is stored in our database (Supabase) and isolated per user.
  • Google refresh tokens are encrypted at rest and are handled only by our server, never exposed to the browser.
  • Access is scoped to your authenticated account; other users cannot read your data.

Data retention and deletion

  • Disconnecting Google. Using "Disconnect" in the dashboard stops syncing, revokes Aptron's access to your Google account, and removes the Google-mirrored events Aptron stored for you.
  • You may also revoke access anytime at myaccount.google.com/permissions.
  • To delete your account and associated data, contact admin@aptron.com.

Third-party processors

  • Supabase — authentication and database.
  • Vercel — application hosting.
  • Google — Calendar API (only when you connect it).

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy; material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above.
